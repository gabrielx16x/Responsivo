The Dragão do Mar Center

The Centro Dragão do Mar de Arte e Cultura is a cultural complex of the Secretariat of Culture of the State of Ceará that is managed in partnership with the Instituto Dragão do Mar. With more than 1.5 million visitors a year, it is among the most relevant Brazilian cultural centers and is one of the main tourist attractions in Ceará. Centro Dragão do Mar is located in one of the most bohemian neighborhoods in Fortaleza, Praia de Iracema. There are 14,500 square meters of built area to experience art and culture, visiting exhibitions at the Museu da Cultura Cearense, at the Museu de Arte Contemporânea from Ceará and at Multigaleria; marveling at scenic shows at Teatro Dragão do Mar, at Espaço Rogaciano Leite Filho and at Arena Dragão do Mar; watching great movies at Cinema do Dragão; exploring the Universe at the Rubens de Azevedo Planetarium; and still enjoying local shows, national and international, in the Amphitheater Sérgio Motta, in the Auditorium and in Praça Verde.

Around 90% of Centro Dragão do Mar's programming is free or at symbolic prices, with the aim of forming an audience in the different artistic languages. This program brings together, from Tuesday to Sunday, presentations in Circus, Theater, Dance, Literature, Photography, Performance, Music, Cinema and Points of Culture. Offering art and culture options for locals and tourists, in addition to the program composed by local and national producer events.

23 years of history

The architecture of the Dragão do Mar Center is characterized by bold lines, designed by architects from Ceará Delberg Ponce de Leon and Nile Faust. Inaugurated on April 28, 1999, in the old port area of Praia de Iracema, Dragão do Mar now has bars, restaurants, handicraft shops, theaters and other training and cultural centres, such as Caixa Cultural, Porto Dragão and Porto Iracema School of Arts.

A space destined to meet people, to promote and disseminate art and culture, the Dragão do Mar Center was idealized in 1993, by the then Secretary of Culture of Ceará, the journalist and anthropologist Paulo Linhares, and the then governor of the State of Ceará, Ciro Gomes.
